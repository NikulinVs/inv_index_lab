==Events==
*[[503 BC]] &ndash; According to the ''[[Fasti Triumphales]]'', [[Roman consul]] [[Publius Postumius Tubertus]] celebrated an [[ovation]] for a military victory over the [[Sabines]].
* [[686]] &ndash; [[Maya civilization|Maya]] king [[Yuknoom Yich'aak K'ahk']] assumes the crown of [[Calakmul]].
* [[801]] &ndash; King [[Louis the Pious]] captures [[Barcelona]] from the [[Moors]] after a [[siege]] of several months.
*[[1042]] &ndash; [[Edward the Confessor]] is crowned [[King of England]].
*[[1077]] &ndash; The first [[Parliament]] of [[Friuli]] is created.
*[[1559]] &ndash; The [[Peace of Cateau-Cambrésis]] treaty is signed, ending the [[Italian Wars]].
*[[1834]] &ndash; The generals in the [[Greek War of Independence]] stand trial for [[treason]].
*[[1860]] &ndash; The first successful United States [[Pony Express]] run from [[St. Joseph, Missouri]], to [[Sacramento, California]], begins.
*[[1865]] &ndash; [[American Civil War]]: [[Union (American Civil War)|Union]] forces capture [[Richmond, Virginia]], the capital of the [[Confederate States of America]].
*[[1882]] &ndash; [[American Old West]]: [[Jesse James]] is killed by [[Robert Ford (outlaw)|Robert Ford]].
*[[1885]] &ndash; [[Gottlieb Daimler]] is granted a German [[patent]] for his [[engine]] design.
*[[1888]] &ndash; The first of eleven [[Whitechapel murders|unsolved brutal murders]] of women committed in or near the impoverished [[Whitechapel]] district in the [[East End of London]], occurs.
*[[1895]] &ndash; The trial in the [[libel]] case brought by [[Oscar Wilde]] begins, eventually resulting in his imprisonment on charges of [[homosexuality]].
*[[1922]] &ndash; [[Joseph Stalin]] becomes the first [[General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union]].
*[[1933]] &ndash; First flight over [[Mount Everest]], a British expedition, led by the [[Douglas Douglas-Hamilton, 14th Duke of Hamilton|Marquis of Clydesdale]], and funded by [[Lucy, Lady Houston]].
*[[1936]] &ndash; [[Bruno Richard Hauptmann]] is executed for the kidnapping and death of [[Lindbergh kidnapping|Charles Augustus Lindbergh, Jr.]], the baby son of pilot [[Charles Lindbergh]].
*[[1942]] &ndash; [[World War II]]: [[Empire of Japan|Japanese]] forces begin an assault on the United States and [[Commonwealth of the Philippines|Filipino]] troops on the [[Battle of Bataan|Bataan Peninsula]].
*[[1946]] &ndash; Japanese [[Lt. General]] [[Masaharu Homma]] is executed in the Philippines for leading the [[Bataan Death March]].
*[[1948]] &ndash; United States President [[Harry S. Truman]] signs the [[Marshall Plan]], authorizing $5&nbsp;billion in aid for 16 countries.
*  1948   &ndash; In [[Jeju Province]], [[South Korea]], a civil-war-like period of violence and [[human rights abuses]] begins, known as the [[Jeju Uprising|Jeju uprising]].
*[[1955]] &ndash; The [[American Civil Liberties Union]] announces it will defend [[Allen Ginsberg]]'s book ''[[Howl]]'' against [[obscenity]] charges.
*[[1956]] &ndash; [[Hudsonville–Standale tornado of April 1956|Hudsonville–Standale tornado]]: The western half of the [[Lower Peninsula of Michigan]] is struck by a deadly [[Fujita scale|F5]] tornado.
*[[1961]] &ndash; The [[Leadbeater's possum]] is rediscovered in [[Australia]] after 72 years.
*[[1968]] &ndash; [[Martin Luther King Jr.]] delivers his "[[I've Been to the Mountaintop]]" speech.
*[[1969]] &ndash; [[Vietnam War]]: [[United States Secretary of Defense]] [[Melvin Laird]] announces that the United States will start to "[[Vietnamization|Vietnamize]]" the war effort.
*[[1973]] &ndash; [[Martin Cooper (inventor)|Martin Cooper]] of [[Motorola]] makes the [[History of mobile phones|first handheld mobile phone call]] to [[Joel S. Engel]] of [[Bell Labs]].
*[[1974]] &ndash; [[1974 Super Outbreak|The 1974 Super Outbreak]] occurs, the second biggest tornado outbreak in recorded history (after the [[2011 Super Outbreak]]). The death toll is 315, with nearly 5,500 injured.
*[[1975]] &ndash; [[Bobby Fischer]] refuses to play in a [[chess]] match against [[Anatoly Karpov]], giving Karpov the title of World Champion by default.
*[[1981]] &ndash; The [[Osborne 1]], the first successful [[portable computer]], is unveiled at the [[West Coast Computer Faire]] in [[San Francisco]].
*[[1991]] &ndash; [[United Nations Security Council Resolution 687]] is adopted.
*[[1996]] &ndash; Suspected "Unabomber" [[Theodore Kaczynski]] is captured at his cabin in [[Montana]], United States.
*[[1997]] &ndash; The [[Thalit massacre]] begins in [[Algeria]]; all but one of the 53 inhabitants of [[Thalit]] are killed by guerrillas.
*[[2000]] &ndash; ''[[United States v. Microsoft Corp.]]'': [[Microsoft]] is ruled to have violated [[United States antitrust law]] by keeping "an oppressive thumb" on its competitors.
*[[2004]] &ndash; [[Islamic terrorists]] involved in the [[2004 Madrid train bombings]] are trapped by the police in their apartment and kill themselves.
*[[2007]] &ndash; [[TGV world speed record#Record of 2007|Conventional-Train World Speed Record]]: A French [[TGV]] train on the [[LGV Est]] high speed line sets an official new world speed record.
*[[2008]] &ndash; [[ATA Airlines]], once one of the ten largest U.S. passenger airlines and largest charter airline, files for bankruptcy for the second time in five years and ceases all operations.
*  2008   &ndash; [[Texas]] law enforcement cordons off the [[Fundamentalist Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints|FLDS]]'s [[YFZ Ranch]]. Eventually 533 women and children will be taken into state custody.
*[[2009]] &ndash; Jiverly Antares Wong [[Binghamton shootings|opens fire]] at the [[American Civic Association (Binghamton)|American Civic Association]] immigration center in [[Binghamton, New York]], killing thirteen and wounding four before committing suicide.
*[[2010]] &ndash; [[Apple Inc.]] released the [[IPad (1st generation)|first generation iPad]], a [[tablet computer]].
*[[2013]] &ndash; More than 50 people die in [[2013 Argentina floods|floods]] resulting from record-breaking rainfall in [[La Plata]] and [[Buenos Aires]], [[Argentina]].
*[[2016]] &ndash; The [[Panama Papers]], a [[news leak|leak]] of legal documents, reveals information on 214,488 [[Offshore financial centre|offshore companies]].
