'''Abati''' is a surname. It was used by an ancient noble family of [[Florence]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Concise Dictionary of Proper Names and Notable Matters in the Works of Dante|year=1914|publisher=Clarendon Press|location=Oxford|page=1|url=http://www.questia.com/read/5635460/concise-dictionary-of-proper-names-and-notable-matters|author=Paget Toynbee|accessdate=11 September 2013}} {{Subscription required|via=Questia}}</ref> 

Notable people with the surname include:

*[[Antonio Abati]] (died 1667), Italian poet
*[[Baldo Angelo Abati]] (sixteenth century), Italian naturalist
*[[Joaquín Abati]] (1865-1936), Spanish writer
*[[Joël Abati]] (born 1970), French handball player
*[[Megliore degli Abati]] (thirteenth century), Italian poet
*[[Niccolò dell'Abbate]] (1509 or 1512-1571), Italian painter
*[[Reuben Abati]] (born 1965), Nigerian newspaper columnist

==Other uses==
* [[Abati, Iran]], village in Iran
* [[Marauna abati]], species of beetle in the family [[Cerambycidae]]
